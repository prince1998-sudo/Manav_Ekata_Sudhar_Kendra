Our History

Human Unity Social Reform Center (HUSRC) Founded in 2080 B.S. by Surya Vishwokarma Human Unity Social Reform Center is a non-profit organization dedicated to transforming rural communities through education, self-employment, and holistic village development. Our vision is to create a society where every individual, regardless of their background, has equal opportunities to grow, learn, and succeed.

Why We Exist The divide between rural and urban development is one of the most pressing challenges in our society. Many children in villages lack access to basic educational materials and infrastructure, which hinders their ability to learn and succeed. HUSRC exists to erase this disparity by bringing city-level quality education, resources, and development initiatives to villages.

At HUSRC, we believe that true social reform begins with unity and the belief that every individual, regardless of their social or economic background, deserves the opportunity to learn, grow, and thrive. We strive to bridge the gap between rural and urban development by creating an ecosystem where education, skill development, and community empowerment go hand in hand.

    • Organizational Structure

    The Human Unity Social Reform Center (HUSRC) follows a collaborative and community-driven organizational structure that ensures transparency, accountability, and effective decision-making. Our structure is designed to bring together passionate individuals, experts, and community members to work toward a shared mission of rural development, education, and self-empowerment.
    1. Founding Body At the core of HUSRC is the Founding Body, led by our visionary founder, Surya Vishwokarma. This body defines the organization‚Äôs long-term strategies, values, and mission. It also oversees major initiatives and ensures that all programs align with the organization‚Äôs goals of social reform and community empowerment.
    2. Board of Directors The Board of Directors provides guidance, policy direction, and governance to ensure HUSRC remains true to its vision. The board is composed of experienced professionals, community leaders, and educators who work together to review performance, evaluate programs, and approve new initiatives.
    3. Executive Team The Executive Team is responsible for the day-to-day operations of HUSRC. This team includes: Executive Director: Oversees organizational management and ensures all projects are executed effectively. Program Managers: Lead specific initiatives such as education, self-employment, health, and village development. Finance & Administration Officers: Manage financial resources, fundraising, and compliance with non-profit regulations.
    4. Community Outreach and Volunteers Our volunteers and field coordinators form the backbone of HUSRC. They work directly with local communities, schools, and families to understand their needs and implement solutions. Volunteers assist in distributing educational materials, conducting awareness campaigns, and organizing skill-development workshops.
    5. Advisory Council The Advisory Council is made up of experts in education, social work, health, and rural development. They provide technical advice, innovative ideas, and guidance to ensure that our programs are sustainable and impactful.
    6. Local Community Committees To ensure that every program resonates with the people it serves, local committees are formed in each village. These committees collaborate with HUSRC teams, share community needs, and help in the execution of projects on the ground.
